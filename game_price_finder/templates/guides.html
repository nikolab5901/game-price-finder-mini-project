Guides for buyers & sellers

Game Price Finder does not process purchases or listings. Use these notes to interpret what you see here and what to verify on marketplaces yourself.

For buyers

  • Asks ≠ deals. Many figures reflect current listing prices or storefront tags—not guaranteed resale outcomes after fees, tax, or shipping.
  • Always check edition. Base game, GOTY, deluxe, remaster, or bundle SKUs trade at different prices; match the exact product code when possible.
  • Physical vs digital vs keys. Cartridges/discs, Steam licenses, and third-party keys carry different risks (region locks, revocation, account-bound).
  • Use sold comps. On eBay and similar sites, filter to completed / sold listings with the same condition and region before trusting a single ask.
  • PC promos. CheapShark-style data tracks time-limited storefront sales; a low number may expire quickly or exclude your region.
  • Cross-check condition. “Like new,” “CIB,” “loose,” and refurbished mean different things—read the seller’s description and photos.

Detail pages link to external search hubs (eBay, retailers, SteamDB where applicable)—open them to dig deeper.

For sellers

  • Price to sold comps. Anchor your ask to recent sold listings with the same edition, region, and condition—not only the lowest active listing.
  • Disclose clearly. Platform, completeness (box/manual), scratches, DLC redemptions, and whether codes are unused should be explicit to avoid disputes.
  • Photos matter. Clear shots of labels, discs/carts, and any damage build trust and reduce returns.
  • Fees & shipping. Marketplace fees, labels, insurance, and taxes change your net—estimate them before you compare to comps.
  • Authenticity. High-value collectibles attract repros; cite provenance where you can and match known good label/print patterns (verify with specialist guides).
  • Digital keys. Resale policies vary by platform and store; confirm you’re allowed to transfer and that keys are unused.

Numbers on this site are orientation only—not appraisals or listing advice. When in doubt, consult a professional for high-value items.
